## 1. Quotation Legal Nature

### 1.1 Contractual Status
Quotations generated by this system are **invitations to treat**, **NOT offers** themselves. Customer acceptance **does NOT automatically form a contract** — your company's confirmation is still required.

Practical recommendations:
- Sales supervisor reviews amounts/terms/payment before sending
- Even after customer "accepts," obtain **written confirmation** (signed quotation or PI) before converting to SO
- Once `valid_until` passes, re-quote rather than reuse

### 1.2 Quotation Variation Risk
- **clone_quotation**: copies an old quote as new version, but **prices/quantities/terms may have changed**; sales should review
- **update_quotation_status**: marking "accepted" **does NOT equal** customer's written confirmation
- **cancel_quotation**: **irreversible** — must create new quote to replace

### 1.3 Antitrust / Fair Trade Compliance
Differential pricing across customers may involve:
- Taiwan Fair Trade Act §22 (reasonable grounds for differential pricing)
- US Sherman Act §1 / Robinson-Patman Act
- EU Competition Law Art. 102

`explore_pricing_curve_tool`'s what-if results **do NOT constitute legal advice on pricing strategy**; consult legal counsel before deployment.

## 2. Sales Order Legal Nature

### 2.1 SO Contract Force
SO creation is a **delivery commitment to customer**. Hard-write tools affecting this:
- **create_sales_order_with_confirm** — commitment formed
- **update_sales_order_item / delivery** — contract amendment (customer written consent recommended)
- **add / remove sales_order_item** — adding/removing items (same as above)
- **cancel_sales_order** — terminates contract, may trigger penalty

### 2.2 ship_sales_order Risk
Shipping triggers:
- Inventory deduction (affects cost accounting)
- AR booking (affects financial statements)
- Customer delivery commitment (affects service level)

**ConfirmCard is the legal confirmation point**: users must review specific slot values before clicking confirm.

---

## 3. Purchase Order Legal Nature

### 3.1 PO Contract Force
PO becomes a **contract with supplier** once approved/sent:
- **cancel_purchase_order** — may incur supplier penalty (if production already started)
- **update_purchase_order_item** — quantity/price change requires supplier consent
- **update_purchase_order_delivery** — date change requires supplier notification
- **add / remove purchase_order_item** — only in draft status (avoiding contract amendment disputes)

### 3.2 Receiving Goods Compliance
- **receive_purchase_order** triggers AP booking
- Quantity/quality acceptance **is warehouse staff's authority** — AI does not replace
- Shortages/damages should follow supplier contract (erpilot does NOT auto-deduct)

## 4. StockCount Legal Nature

### 4.1 Count as Audit Basis
Count adjustments affect:
- **Cost accounting** (overage → income, shortage → cost)
- **Tax filings** (inventory variation = operating cost)
- **Internal control compliance** (SOX / ISO 9001 / GMP require periodic counts + supervisor review)

### 4.2 Count Responsibility Division
This system's design:
- Warehouse staff: execute count + key in actuals
- Supervisor: review variances + apply adjustments
- CPA: adjust books based on count results

**erpilot's ConfirmCard does NOT replace CPA review**. For external financial reports, follow applicable accounting standards + CPA review.

### 4.3 Variance Reason Classification
`variance_reason` categorization is **for internal analysis only**, does NOT constitute:
- Findings of employee fault (damaged/lost ≠ negligence)
- Accusations of supplier quality (count_error may come from any link)
- Evidence for insurance claims (requires separate professional assessment)

## 5. Reorder Suggestion Legal Nature

### 5.1 Advisory Nature
**reorder_suggestion_tool** and **smart_reorder_with_lead_time_tool** outputs are **algorithm suggestions only**, NOT:
- Automatic PO to supplier (must use create_purchase_order_with_confirm)
- Price/delivery commitments
- Future demand forecasts (use demand_forecasting tool)

### 5.2 Algorithm Limitations
- `smart_reorder`'s daily_burn estimation is **conservative**; may underestimate without sales history
- `lead_time` is configured value, **does NOT reflect** supplier's actual recent performance
- `safety_stock` is fixed value, **does NOT adjust dynamically** with service level changes

Major procurement (e.g., annual bulk materials) must be reviewed by procurement supervisor + finance.

## 6. LLM Interaction Risk

### 6.1 Slot Extraction Errors
When user says "change PO-001 to 200 units," LLM may:
- Extract wrong PO number (e.g., PO-002)
- Extract wrong quantity (e.g., treat "200" as "2000")
- Extract wrong part (if PO has multiple lines)

**ConfirmCard is the defense line**: users must review slot values before confirming.

### 6.2 LLM Rendering Inaccuracy
Read tools' `summary` is LLM-translated, may:
- Over-simplify (e.g., "5 damaged + 5 lost" stated as "10 short")
- Fabricate business reasons (hallucination)

**Recommendation**: review `raw` structured data for critical decisions.
